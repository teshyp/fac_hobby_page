Benefits of Meditation Meditation
Get started 29 June 2022

Be here now

Meditation is the habitual process of training your mind to focus and redirect your thoughts. Your physical and emotional stress can melt away by learning to calm your body and mind. This leaves you feeling better, refreshed, and ready to face the challenges of your day with a healthy attitude. With regular practice over weeks or months, you can experience even greater benefits.

Enhance self-awareness

Some forms of meditation may help you develop a stronger understanding of yourself, helping you grow into your best self. For example, self-inquiry meditation explicitly aims to help you develop a greater understanding of yourself and how you relate to those around you. The idea is that as you gain greater awareness of your thought habits, you can steer them toward more constructive patterns.

Reduce stress

A prolonged state unregulated stress response or such agitation can cause physical damage to every part of the body. Meditation affects the body in exactly the opposite way that stress does—by triggering the body's relaxation response. It restores the body to a calm state, helping the body repair itself and preventing new damage from the physical effects of stress.

5 - 20 minutes a day

Meditation involves sitting in a relaxed position and clearing your mind, or focusing your mind on one thought and clearing it of all others. You may focus on a sound, like "ooommm," or on your breathing, counting, a mantra, or nothing at all. It’s generally necessary to have at least five to 20 distraction-free minutes to spend, though meditation sessions can really be any length.
